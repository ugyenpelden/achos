    🛠️ The FabLab Ecosystem: 2025 Vision & Strategy¶

    Where Creativity Meets Accountability¶

    This guide maps out how we work, how we lead, and how we keep our space amazing. Think of the FabLab not just as a room with tools, but as a living Ecosystem where every part depends on the other.

    Figure 1: Aesthetic Centre

    🌟 1. The Core Philosophy: Learn, Lead, Leave it Better¶

    Our goal is to move from being just users to innovators. We aim for a 7/10 to 10/10 accessibility rating by making the lab the most welcoming place on campus.

    Proactive Leadership: Don't wait to be told. If you know how to use the Laser Cutter, teach a friend!

    Figure 1: Student Led Session

    The Spiral Model: Your first project won't be perfect. Start small, document it, and iterate (improve it) over time.

    Figure 1: Spirial Development

    👥 2. Role Specifications (Who Does What?)¶

    To keep the gears turning, we have specific roles. No one works alone; we work as a team.

    Role

    Responsibility

    Team Lead :¶

    To lead the core team in making decision making.

    2nd Lead :¶

    To support the Team Lead in decision making

    Inventory Lead¶

    Take note of the items borrowed by the students and make sure all the things are place in their place

    The website Inventory must be aligned to physical storage. Make sure the things are arranged well.

    Documentation Reviewer :¶

    Fab Academy Graduates or vertifed users for a specific process.

    Each project group gets a designated box. Everything goes in the box, and the box goes on the shelf. Without a designated box, components will not be provided

    Backed by research on what kind of components do you need and why? Whats the purpose of thise components in your project.

    Questions to Answer¶

    • What will your Project do?
    • Who’s done what beforehand? [Research]
    • What will you design?
    • What materials and components will be used?
    • What will be the estimate cost of your Project?
    • What are your key-question to complete your project?

    📦 4. Physical Organization: The Aesthetic Centre¶

    A messy lab is a can be a sign of work happening in the Lab but accountability must be maintained. We are implementing strict record keeping to keep our space professional and sustainable

    Project Display¶

    The Mini Museum: Completed projects aren't left on tables; they are displayed in a sorted, styled museum area.

    Figure 1: Student Led Session

    The Foot Mat & Cleaning Rule: No mud on laptops! A deep clean happens once a week by the incharges.

    Laptops:¶

    Figure 1: Student Led Session

    Labeling System: * Laptops: Numbered and tracked with stickers. If a student is using the laptop then they will have to toggle on the switch on the Web app. Damaged Laptops must be reported as soon as possible

    Waste: Segregated into Reusable Scraps (for keychains!) and Non-reusable. which can be disposed.

    🛡️ 5. The Do’s and Don’ts (The Core Team Code)¶

    Strict rules ensure creative freedom and accessibility

    ✅ DO:¶

    Independent Use: Incharges can use facilities independently to build their skills.

    Safety First: Always wear goggles/gloves when the machines are humming.

    Report Hazards: If a tool breaks, report it immediately. No blame, just fix it.

    Continuous Learning: Use lab laptops to take online courses and level up.

    Make sure if students are using machine, it is being recorded and they follow proper procedure.

    Make sure all the users who are using the laptop has turned on the toggle switch on the webapp

    Conduct meetings and take minutes of the meeting.

    ❌ DON’T:¶

    No Overnights: The lab closes strictly at 9:30 PM unless if teachers are available

    No Guest Stars: Do not bring unauthorized friends during night study.

    The No-Exit Policy: Laptops and tools never leave the FabLab room.

    Personal Projects: Resources are for educational and innovative growth, not personal chores.

    🚀 6. The Project Journey (Standard Workflow)¶

    Follow these 4 steps to go from Idea to Expert.

    The Pitch: Submit a proposal (Idea + Sketch + Steps + Resources).

    The Approval: A Teachers verifies your plan.

    The Build: Use the tools safely. Log your usage in the Log Book.

    The Legacy: Document your work, return all tools, and display your project in the Mini Museum.

    💡 Pro Tip: Students who consistently complete projects and follow rules earn Verified User status, unlocking advanced machine access!
